What's inside

A crowd-runner with teeth.

Dead-simple to pick up, one-more-run to put down. Every street is a fresh gauntlet of gates, hordes, and wrecks.

Multiplier gates

Grow your crowd on the run.

Split-second choices at every gate — ×3 the squad, +12 recruits, or a bruising −40 HP if you read it wrong. Pick the right lane and a lone leader becomes a marching army.

The horde

Outgun the dead.

Your whole squad auto-fires. Climb from pistol to SMG, shotgun, and minigun, and turn a tide of walkers, runners, and hulking brutes into confetti — while the wrecks pile up around you.

One-finger control

Just drag to steer.

Slide left and right to weave your swarm through gates and around parked wrecks. No buttons, no clutter — the whole run is in your thumb. Easy to start, endless to master.

The city

A hand-painted chibi world.

Fog-drenched avenues, glowing storefronts, curb-parked wrecks, and flickering street lamps — a gloomy little world rendered as one smooth 3D scene that scrolls on forever.

Drizzy boost

A battered van parked in the wreckage — tap it and the whole squad piles in for a few glorious seconds of twin .50 cals. Two walls of lead, exploding wrecks, and nothing ahead survives.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

What kind of game is it?

An endless crowd-runner. Steer a squad of survivors down a zombie-filled street, multiply the crowd through the gates, climb the weapon ladder, and outlast the horde.

Does it work offline?

100%. There's no account, no server, and no tracking — the whole city lives on your phone.

Is it really pay-once?

Yes — $1.99 one time on Google Play. No subscription, no in-app purchases, no ads.

Why is the download big?

Every 3D survivor, zombie, street, and weapon ships inside the download (~160MB), so nothing streams from a server and nothing needs a connection.

Is it scary or gory?

More Halloween than horror. The zombies are chibi cartoons, hits are cartoon hits, and there's no blood or gore.
